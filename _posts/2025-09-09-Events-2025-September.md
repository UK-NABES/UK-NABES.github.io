### In September there were two in-person events at which NABES had a significant presence.  

The first was the Royal Statistical Society International Conference where we had a
poster introducing NABES and also held an invited panel session on [Helping
Statistician’s thrive within the Agricultural, Biological and Environmental (ABE)
Sectors](https://virtual.oxfordabstracts.com/event/40660/program?session=156731&date=%222025-9-2%22). We discussed the challenges and possible solutions to how we can:
1. Attract people to the ABE statistics/quantitative methods community
2. Help quantitative individuals thrive within the ABE sectors
3. Link with other quantitative experts (especially between academia and
applied experts) across the sectors
4. Make the role of quantitative methods scientists more visible within the ABE
sectors

We had great ideas and comments from both the audience and our panellists who
were representing NABES, International Biometrics Society, National Centre for
Statistical Ecology, The International Environmetrics Society and the Environmental
Statistics Section of the RSS.  

The second was the [IBS-NABES Joint Workshop](https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/sci/statistics/news/agri-environment-event2025/) on Statistical Challenges in the
Agri-Environmental Sector that brought together academics working on the
development of statistical theory and methods with professional statisticians in the
agri-environmental sector. Caroline Brophy (University of Warwick) and Nick Schurch
(BioSS) both gave key-note speeches on statistical challenges within different parts
of the agri-environmental sector. The workshop also included discussions on:
* Big data and small sample statistics
* Statistical privacy
* Statistical indicators
* AI and sustainabililty

Both events offered NABES members a valuable chance to meet face to face. For
those who are professionally isolated, attending a conference already knowing
people from feeling part of the NABES community transformed the experience,
making it more supportive and enjoyable.
