The workshop this month was on events highlights and aimed to recapitulate where
a large team of statisticians within an organisation might send one person to an
event, who would then feedback to the rest of the team. In total, we heard about five
events/conferences that took place in the last six months:
* The Environmental and Ecological Statistics Conference, Sponsored by TIES
and ESS RSS, which took place at Lancaster University. Presented by
Eleanor D’Arcy (Environment Agency)
* The RSS International Conference, in Edinburgh, focusing on the NABES
panel discussion “Helping statisticians thrive within the environmental,
agricultural &amp; biological sectors”. Presented by Hayley Carr (Babraham
Institute)
* The IBS-NABES Joint Workshop, jointly organised by BIR and UKNABES.
Presented by Teri North &amp; Jen Dillon (Rothamsted)
* The US-UK Scientific Forum on Measuring Biodiversity for Addressing the
Global Biodiversity Crisis, jointly run by the National Academy of Sciences
(US) &amp; the Royal Society (UK), Washington DC. Presented by Philipp
Boerschsupan
* ISI World Statistics Congress, the Hague. Presented by Andrew Mead
(Rothamsted)

It was great to hear about the interesting sessions that happened across this range
of events, and gain insight into key themes across different areas.
